# SIR in West Bengal: How exclusion of nine million voters could shape state politics
- URL: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy51qg00dezo
- Media: BBC
- Fetch Date: 2026-04-13

Concerns are growing in West Bengal, India, after reports surfaced that approximately nine million voters have been removed from the electoral rolls. The State Election Commission cited technical issues and "duplicate entries," but opposition parties allege a deliberate attempt at voter suppression.

The exclusion of such a large number of voters could significantly impact the upcoming state assembly elections, leading to widespread protests and legal challenges. Human rights groups are calling for an independent audit of the voter registration process.
